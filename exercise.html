How does the 20-20-20 Rule Prevent Eye Strain?

The 20-20-20 rule is a technique that can help prevent eye strain, which can occur from prolonged screen use. The rule involves taking a 20-second break every 20 minutes to focus on something 20 feet away. When we stare at a screen for an extended period, our eyes tend to focus and strain on the same spot, which can lead to fatigue, dry eyes, and headaches. The 20-20-20 rule helps reduce this strain by giving our eyes a break from the screen and encouraging us to look at something in the distance.
Looking away from the screen and focusing on something further away allows the ciliary muscles in our eyes to relax, reducing the strain on our eyes. It also helps to blink more often, which keeps our eyes moist and reduces dryness.
By taking regular breaks and following the 20-20-20 rule, we can help prevent eye strain and keep our eyes healthy, especially when using screens for extended periods.
